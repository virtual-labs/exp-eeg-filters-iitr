## EEG Signal Generation and Filtering Procedure:

<b>STEP 1</b>: Select an EEG Dataset from the dropdown menu. Choose the desired <b>Brain Lobe</b> for EEG recording and adjust the <b>Duration</b> and <b>Sampling Rate</b> sliders as required.
<br>
<br>

<b>STEP 2</b>: Click on the <b>Generate EEG Signal</b> button to visualize the original unfiltered EEG signal.
<br>
<br>

<b>STEP 3</b>: Configure the filter settings in the <b>Filter Design</b> section. Choose the <b>Algorithm</b> (IIR/FIR), select the <b>Filter Type</b> (Low-pass, High-pass, Band-pass, etc.), and set the required <b>Cutoff Frequencies</b> and <b>Filter Order</b>.
<br>
<br>

<b>STEP 4</b>: Click on the <b>Apply Filter</b> button to process the noisy EEG signal. Compare the <b>Filtered EEG</b> signal with the <b>Original EEG</b> signal to evaluate the filterâ€™s performance.
<br>
<br>

<b>STEP 5</b>: Click on the <b>Compute PSD</b> button to visualize the Power Spectral Density (PSD) of the <b>Unfiltered</b> and <b>Filtered</b> signals. Observe the frequency components and compare the filter's effect on the signal's spectrum.
<br>
<br>
